The problem

Hype was a strategy. Past tense.

For two years, AI companies could raise, sell, and hire on capability claims alone. That window is closing. Buyers have been burned by demos that didn't survive contact with production. Procurement teams now assume exaggeration. “Powered by AI” went from differentiator to wallpaper in eighteen months.

What cuts through now is the same thing that has always cut through in a low-trust market: a point of view worth arguing with. A claim about how the world works that your company is willing to stake its name on. Not a feature list — a position.

Most founders can't articulate theirs. Not because they don't have one — because they've never been forced to forge it. That's the work.

Forge your POV. Broadcast your truth. Build your army.

FAQ

Why a flat fee?

Because retainers reward time and I sell outcomes. You know the cost before you start; I know the scope. Aligned incentives, no meter running.

We're pre-revenue. Is this for us?

If you're raising or selling in the next six months, yes — positioning is cheapest to fix before the market forms an opinion. If you're still finding product direction, wait; I'll tell you that on the call.

Do you use AI in your work?

Yes, as leverage — never as the strategist. The POV comes from your company and my judgment. In an AI-saturated market, the human point of view is the scarce asset.

Is everything confidential?

Every engagement starts with an NDA. I don't name-drop clients, and anonymous engagements through intermediaries are fine.

What if we need something custom?

Talk to me. But know that “custom” is usually how scope dies — the sprint exists because constraints produce decisions.
