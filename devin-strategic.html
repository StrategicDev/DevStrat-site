The Problem

Hype was a strategy. Past tense.

For two years, AI companies could raise, sell, and hire on capability claims alone. That window is closing. Buyers have been burned by demos that didn't survive contact with production. Procurement teams now assume exaggeration. "Powered by AI" went from differentiator to wallpaper in eighteen months.

What cuts through now is the same thing that has always cut through in a low-trust market: a point of view worth arguing with. A claim about how the world works that your company is willing to stake its name on. Not a feature list — a position.

Most founders can't articulate theirs. Not because they don't have one — because they've never been forced to forge it. That's the work.

FAQ & Contact

Why a flat fee?

Because retainers reward dragging work out, and hourly billing punishes speed. A flat fee aligns us on one thing: the outcome. You know the number before we start, and the scope doesn't move.

We're pre-revenue. Is this for us?

Sometimes. If you're still discovering the product, positioning is premature. If you have something that works and buyers who can't tell why it matters, that's exactly the problem the sprint fixes. The fit call sorts this out honestly.

Do you use AI in your work?

As a tool, where it helps — never as the strategist. The POV, the judgment, and the hard calls are mine and yours. You're paying for twenty years of pattern recognition, not a prompt.

Is everything confidential?

Yes. NDA before we begin, no exceptions. Your positioning, your internal debates, and your unreleased strategy stay between us. Discretion is a large part of what I've been paid for over two decades.

What if we need something custom?

Say so on the call. The sprint is the core offer, but crisis work, executive counsel, and ongoing advisory exist for a reason. If your situation doesn't fit the standard scope, we'll shape something that does.
